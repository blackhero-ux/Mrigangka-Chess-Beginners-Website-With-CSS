CHESS RULES

CHESS RULES: 1 . Objective of the Game:-
The goal is to checkmate your opponent's king - meaning:
The king is in check (under attack), and there is no legal move to escape.
_________________________________________
2. Chessboard Setup
8x8 board (64 squares).
Each player starts with 16 pieces:
1 King
1 Queen
2 Rooks
2 Bishops
2 Knights
8 Pawns
White moves first.
_________________________________________
3. How Each Piece Moves
♔ King
Moves 1 square in any direction.
Cannot move into check.
♕ Queen
Moves any number of squares in any direction (straight or diagonal).
♖ Rook
Moves any number of squares horizontally or vertically.
♗ Bishop
Moves any number of squares diagonally.
♘ Knight
Moves in an L-shape:
2 squares one direction + 1 square sideways.
Can jump over pieces.
♙ Pawn
Moves forward 1 square.
On first move, can move 2 squares.
Captures diagonally.
Special rules:-
En passant
Promotion
_________________________________________
4. Special Moves
🔹 Castling
Move king and rook at the same time.
Conditions:
King and rook haven’t moved.
No pieces between them.
King not in check.
King does not pass through check.
🔹 En Passant
If a pawn moves 2 squares forward and lands beside your pawn,
You can capture it as if it moved only 1 square.
Must be done immediately on the next move.
🔹 Promotion
When a pawn reaches the last rank,
It must be promoted to:-
Queen (most common)
Rook
Bishop
Knight
_________________________________________
5. Check & Checkmate
Check: King is under attack.
Player must remove the threat by:
Moving the king,
Blocking the attack,
Capturing the attacking piece.
Checkmate: No legal move to escape check, Game over.
_________________________________________
6️⃣ Draw Rules
A game can end in a draw by:
Stalemate – No legal moves, but king not in check.
Threefold repetition – Same position occurs 3 times.
50-move rule – 50 moves without pawn movement or capture.
Insufficient material – Not enough pieces to checkmate (e.g., king vs king).
Agreement – Both players agree to draw.
_________________________________________
7️⃣ Illegal Moves
Moving a piece in a way not allowed.
Leaving your king in check.
Moving through pieces (except knight).
_________________________________________
8️⃣ Touch-Move Rule (Tournament Rule)
If you touch your piece, you must move it (if legal).
If you touch opponent’s piece, you must capture it (if legal).

CHESS NOTATION

Chessboard Coordinates


The chessboard uses a coordinate system:
Files (columns) → labled a to h
Ranks (rows) → numbered 1 to 8
So every square has a name like:
a1
e4
h8
Important:
From White’s perspective:
Bottom-left corner = a1
Bottom-right corner = h1
Top-left corner = a8
Top-right corner = h8
So:
e4 means file e + rank 4.
g2 means file g + rank 2.
White pieces start on ranks 1 and 2.
Black pieces start on ranks 7 and 8.

Algebraic Notation

How to Write Moves (Algebraic Notation)
This is called Algebraic Notation.
Piece Letters:-
King K
Queen Q
Rook R
Bishop B
Knight N
Pawn (no letter)
🔹Basic Move Examples
Pawn move
e4 → Pawn moves to e4
(no letter because it’s a pawn)
Knight move
Nf3 → Knight goes to f3
Queen move
Qg2 → Queen goes to g2
Bishop move
Bb5 → Bishop goes to b5
🔹 Captures
Use x for capture.
Qxg2 → Queen captures something on g2
Nxe5 → Knight captures piece on e5
exd5 → Pawn from e-file captures on d5
🔹 Check & Checkmate
→ Check
Example: Qh7+
→Checkmate
Example: Qh7#
🔹 Castling
O-O → Kingside castling
O-O-O → Queenside castling
🔹 Promotion
Write "="
Example:
e8=Q → Pawn promotes to Queen
exd8=N → Pawn captures and promotes to Knight
🔹 When Two Pieces Can Go to Same Square
You must specify which one:
Nbd2 → Knight from b-file goes to d2
R1e1 → Rook from rank 1 goes to e1
